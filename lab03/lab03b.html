Baltimore?

Baltimore is the 22nd largest city in the US with a population of 2.8 million people. Covering over 239 square kilometers, it is located in Maryland on the Patapsco River near the Chesapeake Bay.

The city is a major seaport and automoblie-shipping point. Its diversified economy also includes healthcare, manufacturing, finance, insurance, and education, being home to many colleges and universities including John Hopkins University and over 100,000 post-secondary students. The Federal Social Security Administraction has its headquaters here.

History

Baltimore area was inhabited by Native Americans for at least 10,000 years. In the early 17th century, the area as hunting grounds by the Susquehannock - their name meaning "people of the muddy river". During the subsequent decades, many were drived away or killed by American colonists' infectious dieseases (such as smallpox).

Baltimore has a rich history dating back 1729. It briefly served as the capital from December 20, 1776 to February 27, 1777 and the American national anthem was first written during the Battle of Baltimore in 1812. Further, the city has the higherst number of public statues and monuments per capita in the country and near one third of the buildings are reserved as historic in the US National register.
